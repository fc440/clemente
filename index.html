Roberto Clemente

The great one

Here is a timeline of his life:

  • 1934 - AUG 18th, 1934-Roberto Clemente is born in Carolina, Puerto Rico.
  • 1952 - OCT 9th, 1952 - Roberto Clemente signed with the Santurce Cangrejeros of the Puerto Rican Winter League. His contract valued at $5,000
  • 1953 - JAN 1st, 1953 - Roberto signs to his first american team. The brooklyn dodgers signed him as a minor leaguer.
  • 1954 - NOV 1st, 1954 - The minor league draft lands Clemente on the Pittsburg Pirates. This is where Robertos career would take off.
  • 1955 - APR 17th, 1955 - Clemente made his Major League debut on this day. He played in Pittsburg and had to deal with much racial tension.
  • 1960 - OCT 5th, 1960 - During 1960 Roberto Clemente helped lead his team to the World Series. The Pirates ended up winning. Despite Robertos great play he finished 8th in the NL MVP voting. He was not too happy with the results.
  • 1961 - OCT 5th, 1961 - In 1961 Clemente won his first Gold Glove and batting title. He would go on to win 12 consecutive gold gloves.
  • 1964 - NOV 14th, 1964 - On November 14, 1964 Roberto Clemente married Vera Zabala in San Juan Puerto Rico. They went on to have 3 children together.
  • 1966 - OCT 6th, 1966 - During the 1966 season Clemente finally wins his first MVP. In what his one of his best seasons he got the award he sought after.
  • 1971 - OCT 17th, 1966 - in 1971 the Pirates won the World Series again, this time Clemente was named the World Series Most Valuable Player.
  • 1972 - SEP 15th, 1972 - During the 1972 season Roberto finished off the month of september by getting his 3000th hit. He was the first Latin American player to do so.
  • 1972 - DEC 31st, 1972 - When Managua was effected by a massive earthquake Clemente wanted to help out. To do so he rode over in a plane that was carrying relief supplies, The plane crashed on December 31st, Clemente did not survive.
  • 1973 - AUG 6th, 1973- One year after his death Roberto Clemente is inducted into the Hall Of Fame. He is the first Latin American to do so. His life will be remembered forever.
